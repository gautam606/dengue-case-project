Emergency Warning Signs — Dengue

Know the Warning Signs — Act Fast

Severe dengue can be life-threatening. Recognizing warning signs early can save lives.

If you or someone shows ANY of these warning signs, seek immediate medical attention at the nearest hospital.
Critical Warning Signs
Bleeding gums or nose
Unexplained bleeding from mucosal surfaces
Blood in vomit or urine
Hematemesis or hematuria — sign of hemorrhagic dengue
Severe abdominal pain
Persistent, intense pain — possible plasma leakage
Rapid or difficult breathing
Respiratory distress from fluid accumulation
Extreme fatigue or restlessness
Altered mental state — sign of shock
Persistent vomiting (3+ episodes in 24hrs)
Dehydration risk and worsening condition
Immediate Actions
If Warning Signs Are Present:
  1. Go to the nearest hospital IMMEDIATELY
    Do not wait — severe dengue progresses rapidly
  2. Stay hydrated
    Drink ORS, water, coconut water, fruit juices
  3. Do NOT take Aspirin or Ibuprofen
    These increase bleeding risk — take only Paracetamol
  4. Rest completely
    Avoid physical activity
  5. Monitor vital signs
    Temperature, pulse, blood pressure if possible
Medication Safety in Dengue
SAFE
Paracetamol

(Acetaminophen)

  • For fever relief
  • For pain management
  • Follow dosage guidelines
AVOID
Aspirin
Ibuprofen

(All NSAIDs)

  • Increases bleeding
  • Reduces platelet function
  • Risk of hemorrhage
Why avoid NSAIDs? Dengue causes low platelet counts. NSAIDs (Aspirin, Ibuprofen) further reduce platelet function, dramatically increasing the risk of dangerous internal bleeding.
Tourniquet Test (Educational)
What is the Tourniquet Test?

A clinical screening test used to assess capillary fragility in suspected dengue patients.

How it works:
  1. A blood pressure cuff is inflated on the upper arm
  2. Maintained at midpoint between systolic and diastolic pressure
  3. Held for 5 minutes
  4. Cuff is released and forearm is examined
What to look for:

Petechiae — tiny red or purple dots under the skin, caused by minor bleeding from capillaries.

Positive result: ≥20 petechiae per square inch (2.5 cm²)

Important: A positive tourniquet test is only a clinical indicator. It does NOT definitively diagnose dengue. Laboratory tests (NS1 antigen, IgM/IgG antibodies, CBC) are required for confirmation.
