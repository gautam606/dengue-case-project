    Tourniquet Test (Educational)

    What is it? A clinical test where a blood pressure cuff is inflated on the upper arm for 5 minutes.

    What to look for: Petechiae — small red/purple dots under the skin caused by minor bleeding.

    Positive result: More than 20 petechiae per square inch may indicate dengue.

    This is only a clinical indicator, NOT a definitive diagnosis. Only a trained medical professional should perform and interpret this test.
    Medication Safety
    SAFE to Take
    • Paracetamol (Acetaminophen)
    • For fever and pain relief
    • Follow dosage instructions
    AVOID
    • Aspirin — increases bleeding risk
    • Ibuprofen — increases bleeding risk
    • All NSAIDs are dangerous in dengue
    NSAIDs reduce platelet function and increase the risk of hemorrhage in dengue patients.
